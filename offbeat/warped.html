Warped Tour

The Final Run

2018

By Rachel Miller

Published 10/14/2018

Time is a fickle thing. It is the one thing that no human can control, and it is the last of control that binds us together. How often we wish we could go back in time and relive moments and undo our mistakes.

This is the journey we experience in Manchester Orchestra’s video for “The Alien.” The song is the the fifth track on the bands 2017 album, “A black mile to the surface.” It is a remorseful tune that tells the story of disgrace in moments of weakness and the longing to take it back, predominately when others are unintentionally harmed.

All of these themes are portrayed uniquely in this music video. You don’t know the story that is about to be told, but it begins with a sense of regret and pain. We are transported to this place of “What did I just do?” As the video plays out, things begin moving in reverse. Time is being taken back, something we all desire to be able to do, but alas only something that can be done in video editing. Things become mended, things are placed upright again, and glass is unshattered.

But alas, none of these things can be done in real life. We can’t undo the mistakes we have made or be unbound by time. Things can change in an instant if we aren’t careful. Life can turn around in an instant and mistakes happen so quickly in the hear of rage. And that to me, is the biggest lesson that can be taken from this video. We need to live our lives thoughtfully and preciously. You never truly know when it is going to be changed drastically. Be present with the moments in your life, because time isn’t stopping. Relinquish control to time, and take whatever life throws at you despite your regrets. You never know what you’re going to get, so be patient and know it goes on.
